01The frame

Most of the decisions that look like AI decisions are not.

Sixty percent of a typical publisher workflow is traditional software: CMS logic, database queries, a sane taxonomy, a reliable build pipeline. Thirty percent is rule-based logic: editorial standards, affiliate disclosures, fact-check gates, brand voice rules. Ten percent is genuine AI work: drafting, translation, summarization, audience clustering, semantic search.

Teams that skip the sort waste money putting language models where a well-scoped query would do the job faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees. Applied to Recurrent, the read looks at how the shared WordPress VIP spine, the commerce stack, and the reader data sit against the tools your editors are already using, and draws a map. Orchestration first. Tool choice second.

02Three hypotheses

Specific bets about where hours are leaking across the portfolio.

No portfolio-wide AI editorial policy across the mastheads. Popular Science, Futurism, The Drive, Task & Purpose, Domino, Outdoor Life, and Field & Stream each compete in SEO categories where AI Overviews and AI-generated competitors compress traffic. Without a shared governance layer, each editor is making ad-hoc procurement and usage calls. The consequence: inconsistent content quality signals to Google, legal exposure on AI-generated affiliate copy, and zero operating leverage from the tools that are already being paid for brand-by-brand.

Affiliate commerce content production is labor-heavy and unevenly structured. BobVila.com, Popular Science reviews, Field & Stream gear guides, and Outdoor Life buying content all require high-volume roundup production. Editorial headcount is down post-layoffs. Output targets are not. Orchestration across research, drafting, fact-check, and CMS publish could compress this workload at the same quality bar, which is the difference between margin and no margin on commerce.

Sponsor data and attribution across MIC and the always-on military brands sit in separate systems. Military Influencer Conference sponsors, Task & Purpose advertisers, and We Are The Mighty clients get reported to from different analytics stacks. A unified sponsor view is the kind of problem an orchestration layer solves in one quarter and that sales teams feel in renewal rates heading into Tampa 2026.
